Joint service between SWFT & WCC. Must be referred by GP, nurse or social worker. Cannot come from family members or self-referral.

Specialist Palliative Care Community Service (Macmillan). Must be referred via form not via telephone.
They can provide;

  • Assessment and care planning for patients with complex palliative care needs
  • Psychological support for the patient and their families or carers
  • Information on disease process, treatment, medication, local and national services
  • Advice on symptom control

  • Amoxicillin

    Antibiotic used to treat bacterial infections

  • B12

    Injection given to address B12 deficiency

  • Cellulitis

    • Skin infection that's treated with antibiotics, can be serious if it's not treated quickly

  • Clexane

    Injection used to treat/prevent blood clots forming inside the blood vessels

  • Diarrhoea
  • Glaucoma

    • A common eye condition - optic nerve becomes damaged caused by fluid building up in the eye. Can lead to loss of vision if not diagnosed/treated early, most common in adults aged 70+

  • Palliative

    • Care provided in last 1-2 years of life

  • Tinzaparin

    Anticoagulant injected into stomach, usually administered in evening

  • Syringe driver

    Small battery-powered pump that delivers medication at a constant rate through a small tube under the skin. Sometimes called a continuous subcutaneous infusion.
